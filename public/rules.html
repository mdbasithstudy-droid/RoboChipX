AI/ML Track Rules

1

Team Size

2–4 participants per team.

2

Hackathon Duration

24 continuous hours.

3

Overnight Stay

At least one member must stay overnight at the venue.

4

Bring Your Own Setup

Bring your own laptop, charger, extension cord, hardware components, and accessories.

5

Framework & Language

Use any AI/ML framework, language, or platform of your choice.

6

Functional Prototype

A functional AI prototype or proof of concept is mandatory.

7

Dataset Sources

Datasets must be legally obtained and properly cited.

8

Disclosure

All tools, APIs, models, and resources used must be disclosed during the presentation.

9

Final Presentation

Final presentation using the provided PPT template is mandatory.

10

Plagiarism Policy

Plagiarism or copied projects will lead to disqualification.

11

Professional Conduct

Follow professional conduct throughout the event.

12

Jury Decisions

Jury decisions will be final.

Chip Design Track Rules

1

Team Size

2–4 participants per team.

2

Hackathon Duration

24 uninterrupted hours.

3

Overnight Stay

At least one team member must stay overnight at the venue.

4

Bring Your Own Setup

Bring your own laptop, charger, extension cord, and accessories.

5

Design Languages & Tools

Use Verilog, VHDL, SystemVerilog, FPGA platforms, and approved EDA tools.

6

Working Design

Projects must have a working design with simulation and verification results.

7

FPGA Implementation

FPGA implementation is encouraged but not mandatory.

8

Design Explanation

Teams must explain their design, architecture, verification, and results.

9

Final Presentation

Final presentation using the provided PPT template is mandatory.

10

Plagiarism Policy

Plagiarism or copied projects will lead to disqualification.

11

Open-Source Credits

Open-source resources must be properly credited.

12

Deliverables Deadline

Submit all deliverables before the deadline.

13

Professional Conduct

Follow professional conduct throughout the event.

14

Jury Decisions

Jury decisions will be final.

Robotics Track Rules

1

Team Size

Each team must have 2 to 4 members.

2

Hackathon Duration

24 continuous hours; at least one or two members must stay at the venue during the overnight session.

3

Bring Your Own Setup

Bring your own laptops, chargers, robot components, sensors, actuators, and tools.

4

Hardware Flexibility

Any microcontroller, development board, motor driver, servo, or robotics platform is allowed.

5

Safety Guidelines

All robots and hardware must follow the safety guidelines given by the organizers.

6

Functional Robot

Every team must present a functional robot or working robotic prototype.

7

Final Presentation

A PPT using the official organizer template is mandatory.

8

Jury Walkthrough

Be ready to walk the judges through your robot's design, how it works, and the logic behind it.

9

Originality & Safety

Keep it original – copying or unsafe behavior may get your team disqualified.

Important Notes

Before participation: Ensure your team reads and understands all rules for the selected track. Ignorance of any rule is not an excuse for violation.

During the hackathon: Follow all instructions given by event coordinators and organizers. Any violation may lead to penalties or disqualification.

Questions? Reach out to the organizing committee before the event if you need clarification on any rules.
